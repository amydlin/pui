coursera redesign

A Coursera redesign that gives its online students the true college experience -- frat parties excluded.

Coursera has done wonders in the online learning community, boasting over 23 million users globally after launching five short years ago. By offering courses on in-depth and niche topics from exceptional schools like Princeton and Stanford, Coursera brings their users top-notch curriculum even if their personal situations don't allow for full-time school. However, their retention rates are less than perfect, with only around 5% of students who register leaving with certification of completion.

In our initial research, we found three types of students that use Coursera: the dedicated student, the casual learner, and the browsing passerby

1. The dedicated student has enough internal motivation to carry them through the course. Regardless of rigor, the DS will finish their work and complete the curriculum.

2. The casual learner has some interest, but doesn't necessarily have enough internal motivation to get him through the course if the work is difficult. The CL will drop the course once the novelty wears off and the work picks up.

3. The browsing passerby thinks he is interested, but has no intention on completing the course. The BP will look around before inevitably moving on.
